Mission

The Humphrey Lab investigates how RNA splicing, cryptic exons, and TDP-43 dysfunction shape the vulnerable circuits of the social brain. We integrate long-read RNA sequencing, single-cell transcriptomics, and human genetic variation to understand risk and resilience in ALS, FTD, and Alzheimer’s disease.

Our goal is to translate mechanistic insight into biomarkers and therapeutic strategies that improve the lives of patients and their families.

Research themes

Research areas

  • Isoform-level mechanisms. We use long-read sequencing to resolve full-length isoforms and cryptic exon usage in human brain tissue.
  • Genetics of splicing. We map splicing QTLs and rare variants that perturb TDP-43–sensitive transcripts.
  • Cell-type specificity. Single-cell approaches reveal how neurons, microglia, and glia contribute to disease trajectories.
  • Social brain networks. We connect molecular changes with socio-emotional phenotypes and circuit vulnerability.

Approach

We work at the intersection of wet lab, computation, and clinical cohorts. Our team collaborates widely to leverage deeply phenotyped brain banks, ancestral diversity, and cutting-edge sequencing platforms.
